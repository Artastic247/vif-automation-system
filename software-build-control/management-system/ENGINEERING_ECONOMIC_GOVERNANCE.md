# ENGINEERING_ECONOMIC_GOVERNANCE.md

## Purpose
Define governance for engineering economics, AI credit burn, operational cost control, rework reduction and sustainable execution.

## Scope
Applies to AI/tool usage, onboarding effort, release effort, audit effort, pilot effort, validator effort, rework, failed repair loops and operational scaling.

## Owner/agent
Credit-Burn Controller owns economic monitoring. System owner approves economic priorities and stop conditions.

## Inputs
AI credit-burn register, operational capacity review, failed repair history, rework records, provider pricing, pilot KPI results, release issues and management-review decisions.

## Activities/checklist
1. Monitor AI/tool usage and cost drivers.
2. Identify repeated failed repair loops and unnecessary rework.
3. Assess cost/risk/value before major repair or automation effort.
4. Define stop triggers for uncontrolled credit/resource burn.
5. Prioritise prevention of rework and drift.
6. Review sustainable workload and operational scaling.
7. Feed lessons learned into prompts, validators, onboarding and governance.

## Outputs/records
Economic review, credit-burn trend, rework trend, stop/defer decision, optimisation action and management-review input.

## Maturity level
M3 requires controlled monitoring of operational economics. M4/M5 require optimisation and predictive planning.

## Evidence required
Credit-burn evidence, rework evidence, failed-loop evidence, workload evidence, optimisation evidence and management-review decisions.

## Linked process
MOP-03 Strategic planning risk and opportunity, MOP-06 Management review, MOP-07 Corrective action and continual improvement, AI credit-burn governance.

## Linked agent/skill/procedure/module
Credit-Burn Controller, VIF Orchestrator, QA Gatekeeper, AI Credit Burn Register and operational capacity governance.

## Interface/control point
Interfaces with AI governance, onboarding governance, release governance, portfolio governance and continual improvement.

## PASS/HOLD/BLOCKED rules
- PASS: operational economics remain sustainable and controlled.
- HOLD: rising rework, burn or overload requires optimisation.
- BLOCKED: uncontrolled burn, repeated failed loops, unsafe shortcutting or economically unsustainable operation.

## PDCA
- PLAN: define economic objectives and controls.
- DO: execute governed engineering work.
- CHECK: review burn, rework and sustainability.
- ACT: optimise governance, prompts, validators, onboarding or workload.

## Audit frequency
During management review and after significant burn/rework events.

## Automation allowance
Automation must demonstrate measurable reduction in controlled rework and operational risk before expansion.

## Escalation
Escalate uncontrolled burn, repeated failed repairs, operational overload or unsustainable dependency.

## Update trigger
Burn spike, failed repair loop, pricing change, overload, audit finding or lesson learned.
