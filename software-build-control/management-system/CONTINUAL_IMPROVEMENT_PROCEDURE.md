# CONTINUAL_IMPROVEMENT_PROCEDURE.md

## Purpose
Define the continual-improvement process for the Software Build Control System so audit findings, RCA/CAPA results, lessons learned, KPI trends, AI/prompt failures, validator findings and management-review decisions become controlled improvement actions.

## Scope
Applies to all MOP, COP and SOP processes, agents/workers, skills/work instructions, prompts, AI controls, validators/checks, app onboarding later, release/rollback routes, tenant controls, tool/provider controls and automation-readiness decisions.

## Owner/agent
Lessons Learned Controller owns improvement capture. Process Engineer owns process updates. QA Gatekeeper verifies improvement effectiveness. System owner prioritises strategic improvement actions.

## Inputs
Audit findings, CAPA records, effectiveness reviews, KPI trends, management-review actions, AI bad-output records, prompt failure records, validation reports, customer feedback, support records, maturity assessments and risk/opportunity records.

## Activities/checklist
1. Capture improvement source and classify it.
2. Determine whether the action is correction, corrective action, preventive action, optimisation, standardisation, simplification or automation-readiness improvement.
3. Prioritise by risk, maturity, recurrence, customer impact, release impact, automation impact and resource need.
4. Assign owner, due date and required evidence.
5. Update affected process, skill, prompt, validator, template, agent, tool route or register.
6. Verify effectiveness.
7. Feed high-risk or strategic improvements into management review.
8. Update maturity assessment where evidence supports maturity change.

## Outputs/records
Improvement action, owner assignment, implementation evidence, effectiveness evidence, updated artefact, maturity decision and management-review input where needed.

## Maturity level
M4/M5 require active continual-improvement evidence, trend review and effectiveness verification. Maturity cannot increase where improvement actions are overdue or ineffective.

## Evidence required
Improvement source, risk/priority, owner, action, affected artefacts, implementation evidence, effectiveness review, lesson learned and maturity impact.

## Linked process
MOP-07 Corrective action and continual improvement, MOP-06 Management review, MOP-03 Planning/risk/opportunity, SOP-02 Organisational knowledge control, SOP-12 Lessons learned and knowledge reuse.

## Linked agent/skill/procedure/module
Lessons Learned Controller, Process Engineer, QA Gatekeeper, RCA/CAPA Owner, improvement skill, lessons-learned skill, effectiveness-review procedure and maturity assessment.

## Interface/control point
Interfaces with audit findings, RCA/CAPA, management review, maturity model, process knowledge, prompt register, AI monitoring, validator governance, app onboarding and release governance.

## PASS/HOLD/BLOCKED rules
- PASS: improvement action is implemented, evidence-linked and effectiveness verified where required.
- HOLD: improvement action is open but risk is controlled.
- BLOCKED: critical improvement, repeated failure, ineffective CAPA, maturity overstatement or automation-readiness issue remains unresolved.

## PDCA
- PLAN: identify and prioritise improvement opportunity.
- DO: implement improvement.
- CHECK: verify effectiveness and maturity impact.
- ACT: standardise, update knowledge, adjust maturity and feed management review.

## Audit frequency
Reviewed during internal audits, management review and maturity assessment cycles.

## Automation allowance
Automation is blocked where required improvement actions affecting the relevant route are open, ineffective or unsupported by evidence.

## Escalation
Escalate critical overdue improvement actions, repeated failures, ineffective improvements, maturity overstatement, false PASS risk or automation-readiness gaps.

## Update trigger
Audit finding, CAPA result, effectiveness review, KPI trend, AI/prompt failure, validation finding, release issue, management-review decision, customer feedback or lesson learned.
