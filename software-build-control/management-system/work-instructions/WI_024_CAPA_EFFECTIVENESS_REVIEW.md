# WI_024_CAPA_EFFECTIVENESS_REVIEW

## Purpose
Verify that corrections, corrective actions and preventive actions were completed and are effective in reducing recurrence risk.

## Scope
CAPA actions affecting processes, skills/WIs, prompts, agents, validators, tools, app modules, release controls, security/RLS and evidence controls.

## When to use
Use after CAPA implementation, after a defined monitoring period, after repeat failure, or before closing a significant NC.

## When not to use
Do not use to close CAPA without evidence after use.

## Owner/agent
RCA/CAPA Specialist with affected owner and QA Gatekeeper.

## Inputs
CAPA record, correction evidence, corrective/preventive action evidence, affected artefacts, recurrence data, KPI/result evidence and closure criteria.

## Method steps
1. Confirm correction completed.
2. Confirm corrective action completed.
3. Confirm preventive action where applicable.
4. Confirm affected process/skill/prompt/agent/validator updated.
5. Review evidence after use.
6. Check recurrence risk and actual recurrence.
7. Make effectiveness decision: effective, partially effective or ineffective.
8. Define re-open trigger if ineffective.
9. Update lessons learned and organisational knowledge.

## Outputs/records
Effectiveness review, CAPA closure/reopen decision, updated artefact evidence and improvement input.

## Evidence required
Correction evidence, implementation evidence, updated artefacts, after-use evidence, recurrence check and approval.

## Linked ADP processes
ADP-20 and ADP-21 plus affected ADP process.

## Linked MOP/COP/SOP processes
Clause 10 CAPA/effectiveness, Clause 9 monitoring and management review.

## Linked gates
CAPA closure gate, effectiveness gate, maturity gate and improvement gate.

## Tools allowed
CAPA register, effectiveness register, KPI register, audit findings, lessons learned, knowledge updates.

## Tools prohibited
Closing CAPA from planned action only, no after-use evidence, no recurrence check.

## Risks
Ineffective CAPA, repeated failure, outdated WI/prompt/validator, maturity overstatement, false closure.

## Controls
CAPA cannot close until implementation and effectiveness evidence exist or an approved monitoring HOLD exists.

## Interfaces
RCA/CAPA Specialist, Process Owner, Skill Owner, Prompt Auditor, Validator Auditor, Knowledge Controller, Management Review Owner.

## PASS/HOLD/BLOCKED rules
PASS when effective with evidence. HOLD when monitoring remains open. BLOCKED when ineffective CAPA leaves critical risk uncontrolled.

## Escalation
Escalate ineffective CAPA, recurrence, overdue review, high-risk control failure or maturity overstatement.

## MLA / maturity dependency
Effective CAPA may support maturity recovery; ineffective CAPA can downgrade maturity and block automation/readiness.

## Update trigger
CAPA implementation, review date, recurrence, KPI failure, audit finding or management-review action.
