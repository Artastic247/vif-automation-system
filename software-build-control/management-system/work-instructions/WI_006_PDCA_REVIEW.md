# WI_006_PDCA_REVIEW

## Purpose
Check that every process, job card, app-development activity, audit finding and improvement action follows an explicit Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle.

## Scope
All MOP, COP, SOP and ADP processes, including app builds, repairs, reviews, validation, release, support and improvement.

## When to use
Use during process mapping, job-card planning, audit, RCA/CAPA, management review, release review and continual-improvement review.

## When not to use
Do not use as a slogan or implied statement; PDCA must be evidenced through actions and records.

## Owner/agent
Process Engineer with QA Gatekeeper and Continual Improvement Owner.

## Inputs
Process map, job card, evidence records, audit findings, RCA/CAPA records, improvement actions, KPI data and management-review outputs.

## Method steps
1. Confirm Plan is explicit: objective, scope, risk, owner, method and expected output.
2. Confirm Do is controlled: approved route, assigned role, allowed tools and evidence captured.
3. Confirm Check has evidence: review/test/audit/verification result.
4. Confirm Act updates system: corrective action, lesson learned, knowledge update or procedure/WI change.
5. Confirm PDCA is not merely implied.
6. Confirm audit/RCA/CAPA/continual-improvement loops are active where required.
7. Record PASS/HOLD/BLOCKED decision.

## Outputs/records
PDCA review record, process improvement trigger, evidence gap finding or RCA/CAPA link.

## Evidence required
Plan record, execution record, checking evidence and action/update evidence.

## Linked ADP processes
ADP-01 to ADP-21, especially ADP-15 to ADP-21.

## Linked MOP/COP/SOP processes
Clause 4 process approach, Clause 6 planning, Clause 8 operation, Clause 9 performance evaluation and Clause 10 improvement.

## Linked gates
Planning, execution, verification, validation, release, improvement and management-review gates.

## Tools allowed
Process maps, turtle matrix, job cards, audit reports, NC/RCA/CAPA registers, improvement register and KPI register.

## Tools prohibited
Treating a completed document as proof of PDCA without execution/check/action evidence.

## Risks
Plan not explicit, uncontrolled execution, no evidence check, no system update, repeat failure.

## Controls
A process cannot be considered managed unless all PDCA stages are evidenced or consciously held.

## Interfaces
Process owner, QA Gatekeeper, Internal Audit Specialist, RCA/CAPA Specialist, Continual Improvement Owner and Management Review Owner.

## PASS/HOLD/BLOCKED rules
PASS when Plan, Do, Check and Act are evidenced. HOLD when one element is pending. BLOCKED when critical operation proceeds without plan, evidence check or corrective action route.

## Escalation
Escalate missing Check/Act after audit finding, repeat failure, false PASS or ineffective CAPA.

## MLA / maturity dependency
M1 may define PDCA. M2 must pilot PDCA. M3 must release records. M4/M5 require KPI-driven and improvement-driven PDCA.

## Update trigger
Audit finding, process change, RCA/CAPA, KPI trend, management-review action or repeat failure.
