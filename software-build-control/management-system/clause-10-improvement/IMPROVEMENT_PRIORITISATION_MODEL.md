# IMPROVEMENT_PRIORITISATION_MODEL

## Purpose
Define a controlled prioritisation method for continual-improvement actions.

## Scope
Applies to audit findings, NC/RCA/CAPA outputs, validator gaps, onboarding risks, security risks, prompt failures and automation-readiness actions.

## Owner/agent
Continual Improvement Owner.

## Inputs
Risk severity, likelihood, detection weakness, customer impact, tenant/data/security impact, recurrence risk, compliance relevance, cost/credit burn impact, automation-readiness impact, process maturity impact, ease of implementation, resource availability and due-date requirements.

## Activities/method
Score and classify improvement actions using weighted risk and maturity logic. Escalate critical trends to management review.

## Outputs/records
Prioritised improvement list and escalation decisions.

## Evidence required
Risk evaluation, scoring evidence, approval and implementation evidence.

## Linked process
Clause 10 continual improvement.

## Linked gate
Improvement prioritisation gate.

## MLA / maturity dependency
Higher maturity requires evidence-based prioritisation and trend management.

## Clause 9 input
Audit trends, MLA findings and KPI results.

## Clause 10 output
Approved improvement priority and action sequencing.

## PASS/HOLD/BLOCKED rules
PASS when prioritisation evidence exists. HOLD when scoring/review incomplete. BLOCKED when critical risk lacks prioritisation.

## Escalation
Escalate critical overdue or repeated systemic risk.

## Management-review input/output
Priority trends and critical actions feed management review.

## Organisational-knowledge impact
High-risk lessons update knowledge controls.

## Update trigger
Critical finding, repeat failure or management-review action.
